# Typography-First Logo Generation Spec

Use this reference whenever the user submits words/phrases and expects the skill to generate final title/logo PNGs without additional creative direction.

## Core Intent

Generate a top-tier typographic concept image centered on the submitted text. The primary task is not to make a beautiful background or ordinary poster; it is to make the target text the strongest, largest, clearest, most memorable visual subject.

The text must be seen, read, and felt first. All other elements must serve the text by explaining, strengthening, directing attention to, surrounding, supporting, pressuring, releasing, restricting, extending, or creating a visual double meaning with it.

Default final canvas: `1360x560` transparent PNG. If a different internal composition ratio better fits the word, keep the final exported canvas at `1360x560` and use transparent space outside the chosen composition.

## Hierarchy

The visual hierarchy must be:

1. Target text.
2. Core metaphor elements around or inside the text.
3. Atmosphere, texture, detail.

Never let background, people, props, buildings, scenery, light, or decorative elements overpower the text.

## Semantic Interpretation First

Before prompting, analyze the target text:

- Is it emotional, conceptual, philosophical, object-based, historical, comic, romantic, technological, documentary, mythic, or brand-like?
- Is its pressure gentle, strong, repressed, solemn, absurd, calm, dangerous, poetic, mysterious, sharp, chaotic, or ceremonial?
- Which visual tradition fits the meaning: Eastern calligraphy, modernism, futurism, classicism, minimalism, surrealism, expressionism, constructivism, luxury editorial, experimental typography, documentary archive, or another style?
- What is the deeper metaphor beyond the most obvious literal symbol?
- What should be the first emotional impression?
- Should the spatial motion expand, compress, rise, fall, surround, fracture, converge, dissolve, drift, lock, or loop?

Avoid shallow cliches. For example:

- Freedom is not just blue sky or birds; it can be dissolved boundaries, loosened structure, expanded space, reduced gravity, or escape from a frame.
- Desire is not just red fire; it can be attraction, pull, possession, emptiness, temptation, and unstoppable approach.
- Control is not just locks or grids; it can be measurement, discipline, coordinates, permission, borders, allowed/forbidden states.
- Loneliness is not just one person; it can be missing response, scale imbalance, excessive blankness, unreachable sound, broken relation.
- Anxiety is not just warning red; it can be future pressure, misalignment, information density, compressed breathing space.
- Civilization is not just cities or columns; it can be order, memory, writing, institutions, archives, myth, scale, accumulation, and ruins.
- Time is not just a clock; it can be afterimage, repetition, disappearance, overprint, erosion, delay, echo, and irreversibility.
- Depth is not just a black hole; it can be layers, hidden interior space, unseen bottom, and visual progression from surface inward.

The result should be the visual thought of the word, not an illustration of the word.

## Text Design Requirements

The text must not be a normal font placed on top of decoration. The letterform itself must express the meaning.

Use meaning-driven changes such as:

- Stroke proportion, weight, rhythm, compression, expansion.
- Local fracture, erosion, stretching, dragging, growth, folding, or suspension.
- Negative-space imagery inside letters.
- Edges affected by force, texture, rules, time, pressure, or release.
- Text as container, architecture, object, body, machine, map, system, window, trap, monument, or living structure.

Keep it readable.

For Chinese:

- Preserve every character exactly.
- Respect basic character structure.
- Do not create fake Chinese, garbled characters, missing strokes, extra strokes, or wrong characters.
- Use calligraphy, inscription, geometric title type, experimental lettering, brand type, architectural type, or sculptural type when appropriate.
- Let radicals, center of gravity, stroke thickness, and spatial structure feel custom-designed.

For English/numbers:

- Preserve exact spelling, capitalization, and numerals.
- Do not add, omit, or alter letters/numbers.
- Control spacing, weight, proportion, baseline, rhythm, and negative space like a premium title asset.
- Distort, cut, compress, stretch, overlay, or hollow only while preserving readability.

## Metaphor System

Do not make the image text-only unless the word truly demands it. Build a small, precise metaphor system around the text using only elements that serve the text:

- Symbolic objects, abstract graphics, geometry, spatial structures, natural elements, machines, architecture, bodily metaphors, light, material shifts, color conflict, sign systems, negative space, repeated afterimages, mirrors, fractures, expansion, compression, floating, falling, burning, freezing, dissolving, growing, locking, or dispersing.

Use few strong elements rather than many weak ones. One accurate metaphor is better than ten decorations.

Never make auxiliary elements into an independent illustration. Never use random decoration.

## Style Selection

Do not use one fixed style. Select style from the word's meaning. Possible directions include:

- High-end minimalist graphic design.
- Constructivist poster composition.
- Swiss grid system.
- Contemporary Japanese graphic design.
- Eastern calligraphy with modern layout.
- Luxury editorial visual.
- Futurist technology visual.
- Classical inscription with modern design.
- Surreal concept poster.
- Avant-garde magazine cover.
- High-end cultural exhibition poster.
- Abstract expressive typography.
- Neo-Bauhaus geometry.
- Art book cover.
- Sculptural typography installation.
- High-contrast graphic design.
- Poetic blank-space visual.
- Ordered concept collage.
- Cold rational systematic design.
- Emotionally intense impact poster.
- Premium brand key visual.
- Art photography fused with type.
- Experimental poster.
- Architectural typography.
- Visual-poetry typography.

Style must come from meaning, not from a template.

## Composition

Use a professional, high-impact composition that protects text priority:

- Giant text dominant: text takes at least 50% of visual area.
- Monumental center: text as core totem.
- Minimal blankness: huge restrained text with precise metaphor details.
- Pressure composition: elements press inward.
- Release composition: text expands outward, elements open or fracture.
- Layered composition: text is front/midground and unmistakable.
- Graphic surround: circles, frames, lines, arrows, ripples, or symbols form a system around text.
- Cropped scale: huge letters crop at edges for impact.
- Negative-space metaphor: hidden second meaning inside/around letters.
- Contrast composition: order vs chaos, freedom vs restriction, life vs machine, etc.

Avoid small corner text, generic title placement, random distribution, empty center, unclear hierarchy, and template-like cover layout.

## Ratio and Size

Choose the internal composition ratio from the word's spatial tendency, then export on a `1360x560` transparent canvas unless the user asks otherwise.

- Square/near-square: core, closure, stability, totem, power, order, belief.
- Vertical: rise, fall, pressure, tower, abyss, judgment, spiritual height.
- Horizontal: flow, expansion, distance, journey, spread, freedom, connection, time line.
- Ultra-wide: epic, civilization, migration, universe, history, macro systems.
- Ultra-vertical: abyss, extreme loneliness, tower, cliff, faith, depth.
- Banner: speed, sound, road, river, track, information flow, breath, wind.

Whatever ratio is chosen, text must remain large, clear, dominant, and compositionally complete.

## Color, Light, and Material

Choose color and light from the word. Avoid fixed bright/dark treatment, cheap palettes, dirty colors, meaningless gradients, vulgar neon, oversaturation, and random AI color.

Usable directions include high-contrast black/white, red/black, cold blue/silver gray, ivory/gold, rice white/ink, deep green/copper, orange-red/cream, purple-black/silver, gray-white with precise fluorescent accent, monochrome plus one accent, large flat color plus a high-saturation detail, or controlled premium gradients.

Light must strengthen text, not become spectacle.

Choose material from meaning: paper, ink, metal, stone, glass, silk, liquid, smoke, dust, plant fiber, machinery, digital grid, old photo, print grain, torn paper, transparent layers, light lines, collage, sculpture, embossing, cracks, mirror reflection, vector geometry, or precise color blocks.

Material must not overpower letterform structure.

## Final QA

Accept only if:

- Text is first-read and dominant.
- Text is accurate and readable.
- Text is custom-designed, not default font.
- Text itself expresses meaning.
- Metaphor elements clearly serve the word.
- Style, color, composition, and ratio match the meaning.
- Auxiliary elements do not steal attention.
- No extra words, watermark, signature, logo, or QR code.
- No fake text, wrong characters, missing or extra letters/strokes.
- No background; final PNG has alpha.
- No cheap 3D, vulgar neon, Canva/PPT/ad-template feeling, low clarity, blur, jagged edges, or random AI texture.
